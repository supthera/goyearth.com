Archive

Primary sources, essential texts, and commentary

  • Zetetic Astronomy — Earth Not a Globe

    Samuel Birley Rowbotham · 1865

    The foundational text of the modern flat earth movement. Rowbotham details his Bedford Level experiments and builds a systematic, observation-based case against the spherical earth model. Essential reading for understanding the empirical roots of zetetic philosophy.

  • Zetetic Cosmogony — A Stationary Plane Circle

    Thomas Winship · 1899

    A rigorous continuation of the zetetic tradition, presenting cosmological arguments for a plane earth grounded in direct observation and experiment. Winship challenges the foundations of Newtonian astronomy and offers an alternative framework for understanding the heavens above a stationary, flat earth.

  • Is the Bible From Heaven? Is the Earth a Globe?

    Alexander Gleason · 1890

    Gleason — the same cartographer behind the celebrated 1892 map — interweaves scriptural exegesis and zetetic science to argue that the Bible describes a flat, stationary earth. A companion to his patent work, this volume makes the theological and cosmological case together, drawing on Genesis, Job, and the Psalms alongside observational evidence.

  • Gleason's New Standard Map of the World — Patent

    Alexander Gleason · 1892

    The original patent filing and accompanying map that form the basis of this project. Gleason's azimuthal equidistant projection places the North Pole at the centre of a disc, with Antarctica forming the outer ring — a layout that matches the flat earth model with striking precision.

  • Terra Firma — The Flat Earth Examined

    David Wardlaw Scott · 1901

    Scott expands on Rowbotham's work, marshalling scripture, science, and surveying records to argue that the earth is a stationary plane. Particularly compelling are his chapters on the behaviour of rivers and the impossibility of water conforming to a convex surface.

  • One Hundred Proofs That the Earth Is Not a Globe

    William Carpenter · 1885

    A systematic catalogue of one hundred distinct arguments against the spherical earth model, drawn from observation, geometry, and common sense. Carpenter presents each proof concisely, making this an accessible companion to Rowbotham's more comprehensive treatise.

  • Kings Dethroned

    Gerrard Hickson · 1922

    A methodical refutation of the Copernican system and the theories of Newton and Einstein. Hickson argues from first principles and direct observation that the earth is stationary and flat, challenging the astronomical establishment with questions that remain unanswered by orthodox science.

  • Is the Newtonian Astronomy True?

    William Carpenter · 1897

    A pointed companion to Carpenter's earlier catalogue of proofs, this pamphlet targets Newtonian astronomy directly — examining its logical foundations and exposing the assumptions that underpin the heliocentric model.

  • The Sea-Earth Globe

    Zetetes · 1899

    A concise zetetic tract examining the behaviour of water at rest and in motion as evidence that the earth's surface is level. Published under the pen name Zetetes — Greek for "one who seeks" — in the tradition of empirical flat earth enquiry.

  • Adrian Galilio

    Lady Blount · 1903

    A novel by the prolific flat earth advocate Lady Blount, weaving zetetic philosophy into narrative form. One of the few works of fiction in the canon, using the story of Adrian Galilio to dramatise the conflict between received astronomy and observational truth.

  • Zetetic Astronomy — Earth Not a Globe

    Lady Blount & Albert Smith · 1904

    A revised and expanded edition of zetetic astronomy produced by Lady Blount and Albert Smith, updating Rowbotham's foundational arguments with new experiments and observations gathered by the Universal Zetetic Society.

  • Is The Earth A Globe Whirling In Space?

    Karl A. Smith · 1909

    A systematic examination of the globe model's central claims — rotation, orbit, and gravitational mechanics — subjected to zetetic scrutiny. Smith applies direct observation and common-sense geometry to question each pillar of conventional astronomy.

  • Foundations of Many Generations

    E. Eschini · 1917

    Drawing on scripture and natural observation, Eschini presents the earth as a fixed, level plane — the immovable foundation described throughout the Biblical record. A devotional and cosmological argument in the zetetic tradition.

  • Heaven and Earth

    Gabrielle Henriet · 1953

    One of the last major works of the classical flat earth tradition, published in the mid-twentieth century. Henriet surveys cosmological, astronomical, and meteorological evidence to argue that the earth is stationary and the heavens rotate above it on a fixed plane.

  • The Greatest Lie on Earth

    Edward Hendrie · 2016

    A comprehensive modern synthesis of flat earth evidence, drawing on classical zetetic sources alongside contemporary scientific claims. Hendrie argues that the globe model is a deliberate deception, marshalling scripture, physics, and the testimony of the senses in a thorough refutation of the moving-globe hypothesis.

  • The Enlightenment of the World

    Abizaid, John George, 1868

    A primary source document from the flat earth canon, presenting cosmological and philosophical arguments in favour of a plane earth. Preserved here as part of the archive's collection of essential zetetic literature.
