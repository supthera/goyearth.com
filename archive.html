Primary sources, essential texts, and commentary

  • Zetetic Astronomy — Earth Not a Globe

    Samuel Birley Rowbotham · 1865

    The foundational text of the modern flat earth movement. Rowbotham details his Bedford Level experiments and builds a systematic, observation-based case against the spherical earth model. Essential reading for understanding the empirical roots of zetetic philosophy.

  • Zetetic Cosmogony

    Thomas Winship · 1899

    A rigorous continuation of the zetetic tradition, presenting cosmological arguments for a plane earth grounded in direct observation and experiment. Winship challenges the foundations of Newtonian astronomy and offers an alternative framework for understanding the heavens above a stationary, flat earth.

  • Gleason's New Standard Map of the World — Patent

    Alexander Gleason · 1892

    The original patent filing and accompanying map that form the basis of this project. Gleason's azimuthal equidistant projection places the North Pole at the centre of a disc, with Antarctica forming the outer ring — a layout that matches the flat earth model with striking precision.

  • Terra Firma — The Flat Earth Examined

    David Wardlaw Scott · 1901

    Scott expands on Rowbotham's work, marshalling scripture, science, and surveying records to argue that the earth is a stationary plane. Particularly compelling are his chapters on the behaviour of rivers and the impossibility of water conforming to a convex surface.

  • One Hundred Proofs That the Earth Is Not a Globe

    William Carpenter · 1885

    A systematic catalogue of one hundred distinct arguments against the spherical earth model, drawn from observation, geometry, and common sense. Carpenter presents each proof concisely, making this an accessible companion to Rowbotham's more comprehensive treatise.

  • The Enlightenment of the World

    Abizaid, John George, 1868

    A primary source document from the flat earth canon, presenting cosmological and philosophical arguments in favour of a plane earth. Preserved here as part of the archive's collection of essential zetetic literature.
